History of Architecture

Us Capitol

The history of the United States Capitol Building begins in 1793. Since then, the U.S. Capitol has been built, burnt, rebuilt, extended and restored. The Capitol that we see today is the result of several major periods of construction; it stands as a monument to the ingenuity, determination and skill of the American people.

Cologne Cathedral

Cologne Cathedral (German: Kölner Dom) is a monumental church dedicated to Saint Peter, renowned for its twin spires, intricate Gothic architecture, and immense size. It is one of the largest cathedrals in Europe and a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Sagrada Familia

The Sagrada Família is a one-of-a-kind temple, for its origins, foundation and purpose. Fruit of the work of genius architect Antoni Gaudí, the project was promoted by the people for the people. Five generations now have watched the Temple progress in Barcelona. Today, more than 140 years after the laying of the cornerstone, construction continues on the Basilica.

Notre Dame de Paris

The Notre Dame Cathedral is a testament to architectural innovation, spiritual influence, and human ingenuity. Its construction, spanning several centuries, showcases the evolution of Gothic architecture and mirrors Paris's flourishing economy and cultural development. By exploring the cathedral's architecture, construction, and history.

Pisa Complex

The Pisa Complex is a group of buildings in Pisa, Italy, including the famous Leaning Tower of Pisa. The complex is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and represents a unique example of medieval architecture and engineering.
